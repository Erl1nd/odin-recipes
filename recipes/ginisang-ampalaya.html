Ginisang Ampalaya

Ginisang Ampalaya (Sauteed Bitter Melon)

The thought we often associate with “ampalaya” is bitterness and a generally unpleasant taste. And while it is true that this vegetable holds this flavor— after all, it is called a bitter melon— it doesn’t always have to taste unfavorable. Some ampalaya can initially have that overpowering bitterness, but with the right preparations, it can actually taste satisfyingly flavorful and delicious. A classic dish that utilizes this unique flavor is Ginisang Ampalaya.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium ampalaya
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 onion, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 2 large Roma tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1/2 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions

  1. Cut ampalaya lengthwise and with a spoon, remove seeds and scrape off white pith. Sliced thinly and place in a bowl of cold water until needed. Drain well when ready to use.
  2. In a wide skillet over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic. Cook, stirring regularly, until softened.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook, mashing with the back of the spoon, until softened and release juice.
  4. Add fish sauce and cook for about 1 minute.
  5. Add shrimp and continue to cook, stirring occasionally, until shrimp starts to change color.
  6. Add water and bring to a boil.
  7. Add ampalaya and gently toss to combine. Cook for about 2 to 3 minutes or until tender yet crisp.
  8. In a thin stream, add eggs and gently stir to distribute. Continue to cook for about 1 minute or until eggs have set.
  9. Season with sugar, salt, and pepper to taste. Serve hot.
